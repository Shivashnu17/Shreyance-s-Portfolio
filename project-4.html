Overview

This project critically analyzes the intersection of fashion and business by examining Allen Solly's product category through fashion marketing, buying, and trend forecasting. Utilizing insights from WGSN, key SS26 trends were identified to inform the development of a new collection aligned with emerging consumer preferences and market demands.

Through comprehensive Allen Solly brand research and competitive analysis, a market-responsive collection was developed, integrating trend-driven aesthetics, commercial viability, and brand identity.

Concept Note

Allen Solly’s SS26 Casual Striped Shirts collection, titled "Wear your way, everyday," celebrates inclusivity and versatility, offering a timeless, sustainable solution for men of all ages. Crafted from organic and recycled cotton, these shirts provide comfort, durability, and style that seamlessly adapts to every life stage—from business meetings to college life to coffee with childhood friends. The collection highlights how these shirts cater to men in their 20s, 40s, and 60s, emphasising a style that has no age limit and can be carried by men of all ages. Catered towards fashion-conscious men seeking sustainable, durable, and comfortable clothing that works for any occasion. Through diverse age representation and trendy designs, the collection positions Allen Solly as the go-to brand for modern, sustainable fashion that evolves with every man’s journey.

Reflections

This project bridged heritage and modernity. Immersed in Gaddi artisans’ lives, I witnessed their quiet resilience—craft as survival. Synthetic fakes sparked urgency: How to value “slow” in a “fast” world? Raw moments—calloused hands, clattering looms—taught me authenticity isn’t staged; it’s unearthed by listening, not filming.

Challenges

The rugged terrain of Naggar tested our teams limits—batteries dying in sub-zero temperatures, wind drowning out interviews, and gaining trust in communities wary of outsiders exploiting their stories. Balancing rawness with a narrative tightrope for Gen Z audiences was tricky. How do we avoid romanticizing poverty while celebrating craft? Editing became an emotional battleground: cutting poignant scenes to keep pacing sharp felt like losing pieces of their souls. The biggest hurdle? Translating “sustainability” from a buzzword into a visceral feeling—making viewers care beyond a hashtag.

Key Takeaway

Trust is the first draft: Relationships matter more than shots. Without the artisans’ vulnerability, the film would lack its heartbeat. Imperfection tells truth: Flawed audio, shaky frames—these “mistakes” became the film’s texture, mirroring the craft’s handmade essence. Storytelling is activism: Data (government certifications, thermal science) validates, but emotion mobilizes. A single artisan’s pride can outshine a thousand statistics. The future is hybrid: Tradition thrives when woven into contemporary contexts—like Gaddi wool reimagined as streetwear.
